Detecting sluice gates using machine learning

This project aims to apply machine learning to real raw data, rather than data for standard training such as MNIST.

In our group, we obtained radar measurement data of ships passing through the sluice gate from a company where our group members did internship. We applied machine learning to these data. The measurement data of the sluice gate is shown in the upper right. It is impossible to determine which measurement point is located pm the movable part (gate), side wall, and other parts of the sluice gate. Our model was trained to automatically determine them.

Development of Aero-Cover for Road Bike

More than 80 percent of a bicycle's resistance is aerodynamic drag. On today's high-end road bikes, the cables and wires that create aerodynamic drag are stored inside the frame. However, you can't store the cables in the frame you already own, which often leaves you far behind other riders in terms of equipment. Therefore, I developed a aero-cover to reduce the aerodynamic drag of these cables through CFD and wind tunnel testing.

Analysis of MILD Combustion Field

As more efficient and environmentally friendly combustors are required, MILD combustion is one of the promising solutions. MILD combustion is characterized by a wide range and complex shape of chemical reactions, which makes the development of combustors using this combustion mode difficult. In order to simulate a combustor with MILD combustion easily, a highly accurate model of MILD combustion is required. A basic study to create such a model was carried out. This research was also published as a chapter in a paper.
